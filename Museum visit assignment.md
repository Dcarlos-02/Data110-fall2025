# Global warming 

Another interesting discovery includes the display of graph that shows information about global warning and energy consumption.

![Alt text](https://github.com/Dcarlos-02/Data110-fall2025/blob/1037eb1120f420960a8ef2c2f41b70241bc7c2e3/museum%20pics/Screenshot%202025-12-03%20185843.png)

Above, we have a graph that shows the trend of energy utilization throughout the day. It intends to help balance solar power with energy demand. 
On the x-axis we have different time of the day. On the y-axis we have the amount of power generated in gigawatts. The graph is a line plot that shows the trends within three categories. 
The green line represents the natural gases, the purple line represents solar energy, and the gray line represents the other sources of energy. 
As observed, more natural gases are used overnight. The green line reached its peak,approximately 22 gigawatts, after 6PM. 
As for solar energy, since the graph is bell shaped, using the empirical rule, we can conclude that 95% of solar energy is produced between 8AM and 6PM, with its highest level of production around 12PM. 
Finally, as for the other sources, just like the natural gases the highest consumption happens overnight. 

![Alt text](https://github.com/Dcarlos-02/Data110-fall2025/blob/1037eb1120f420960a8ef2c2f41b70241bc7c2e3/museum%20pics/Screenshot%202025-12-03%20185534.png)

The graph above is another line plot that shows the past and prospective trends in global warming starting from the 1900s to 2100.
The research was conducted by several organizations, with each color representing a different organization. 
The red line represents the center for climate system research.
The orange line represents the Canadian center for climate modeling and analysis.
The turquoise line represents the Geophysical fluid dynamical laboratory. 
The purple line represents the National center for atmospheric research. 
Overall, it is observable that the world will experience a major temperature rise, which will cause extreme heat, drought and even mass extinction.
